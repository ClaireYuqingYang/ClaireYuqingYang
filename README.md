# Claire Yang

MS in Applied Statistics for Social Science Research, New York University
Research Assistant, Columbia Business School

## About

I am interested in how individuals interpret and engage with information in digital environments. My work combines causal inference and computational methods to study behavior on online platforms, with a particular focus on misinformation, attention, and decision-making.

## Current Work

**FeedFrame (ongoing)**
A browser-based experimental system for studying engagement with misinformation on social media platforms.

* Designed and implemented an end-to-end experimental pipeline integrating Qualtrics, oTree, and a browser-based interface
* Collected high-resolution behavioral trace data (e.g., clicks, shares, dwell time, scroll behavior)
* Conducted empirical analyses using mixed-effects models, difference-in-differences, and cluster-robust inference
* Focused on identifying causal effects of interface-level interventions on user engagement and content sharing
